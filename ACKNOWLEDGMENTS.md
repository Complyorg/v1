# Acknowledgments

## Origin

The Comply.org Attestation Standard has its roots in work originally carried out
by **PrivacyCloud SL**, a Spanish technology company that wrote the core standard
in 2018 and eventually published the current comply.org website.

PrivacyCloud's original vision was to create an open, vendor-neutral framework
through which any organisation could publicly attest to its data-protection
practices. That vision — transparency as a default, machine-readable compliance
data, and traceable but privacy-respecting expert verification — remains the
foundation of every version of this standard.

## Evolution

The standard has since evolved through contributions from privacy engineers,
legal practitioners, and open-source developers. In 2026 the specification was
formalised as an open standard under the **comply-org** GitHub organisation,
with the goal of enabling any platform, auditor, or third-party reviewer to
produce and publish Comply.org attestation pages.
